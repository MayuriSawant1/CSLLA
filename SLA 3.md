Stage 2: Activity 2 – Video Creation


Video Topic: SIEM Tools and Threat Detection in a SOC Environment


Learning Process (250–300 words):
The video was created on the topic "SIEM Tools and Threat Detection in a SOC Environment," which directly aligns with the course subject of Cyber Security Laws and Legal Aspects, particularly regarding the lawful monitoring, evidence collection, and incident response obligations under the IT Act 2000 and related regulations.
Topic selection began with a review of the Infosys SpringBoard course modules on Internet Crimes and Cyber Security, where SIEM emerged as the most practically relevant and legally significant area. Key resources consulted included NIST SP 800-92 (log management guidelines), MITRE ATT&CK Navigator, Splunk documentation, and academic papers on SOC maturity models.
The script was structured in three acts: (1) introduction to SOC architecture and legal mandates for incident logging, (2) a walkthrough of how a SIEM system ingests, correlates, and alerts on log data, and (3) a practical demonstration using Splunk Free Trial showing a simulated brute-force attack detection.
The video was produced using OBS Studio for screen recording, PowerPoint for slides (with embedded architecture diagrams), and DaVinci Resolve for editing and captions. Diagrams illustrating the SIEM data-flow pipeline and the MITRE ATT&CK kill chain were created in draw.io and embedded as visual aids.
Key highlights of the presentation include a live Splunk dashboard walkthrough, explanation of alert triage workflow, and discussion of how SOC findings serve as admissible digital evidence under Section 65B of the Indian Evidence Act — bridging technical practice with legal frameworks relevant to the course.
